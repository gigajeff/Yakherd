A corrupt task-A record naming task B's Job, or a legacy record without coherent evidence, could therefore terminate B even when classified `OWNERSHIP_RECORD_INCONSISTENT` or `OWNERSHIP_UNVERIFIED`. This violated the amendment's non-action rule. Required correction: exact task/Job-name coherence before open/terminate, warning retirement on mismatch, and a direct regression.

Broker cleanup also closed the exact Job before fallback membership verification.

The hot-CPU test injected error text directly into `_cleanup_severity` and did not exercise the real path. This violated required test 7 and the three-part blocker rule. Required correction: explicit failure for unsuccessful waits/residual members, exact Job authority through final verification, and a cleanup-path regression.

#### P1-3: Explicit resume preserves a stale embargo after the concrete blocker is gone

`resume_warning` freshly classified processes/severity but refused every historical `state == cleanup_failed` even with no current verified owned process or hazard (`cycle-1 source:2131-2135`). Its test created exactly that no-hazard record and expected denial.

This contradicted automatic resume after resolution/downgrade and current-concrete-hazard-only blocking. Required correction: deny only when fresh evidence satisfies the full blocker conjunction, retire the stale state otherwise, and test both resolved and current blockers.
